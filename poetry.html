To the Night Woman

Silence thy bird of prey might-less god
for my love is passing by.
Her name is Not-I, and she wears a cloak of night.
Reverend Lady, Mistress of the Night, White Child,
May only the True follow you and kiss your brow.

Become the word that empowers you,
Crave deprave. Surrender. Us under. Ground surround. Sorrow by morrow.
Death, my end, Not-I, my Goddess.
Be sinful, be immoral, be wicked,
Be strong and proud, be the huntress, be a wo-without the-man, be self.
Even if you hear:
"Death be not proud."



43

Tree branches block the light
And govern truth.
I cannot bring myself to write your names of stone
With a hand that wriggles on this page.
Sweet seedlings, I have heard
Mexican fields bare strange flowers
Blood on the petals
And blood at the roots.
Brown shoots shrivel in the Southern heat.
Strange flowers, indeed, growing out of planted weeds.
Northern breeze,
Complicity does tax.
Forced migrations
Of military means,
Tanks, guns, bullets and death.
Spring seedlings
I refuse to write your names
In the language of the plow
Or the flowerpots.



The Gift

Rising peak in sight
A last step before the summit.
But it slithers second to second masking time interminably.
The touch,
Humanity’s touch: impersonal, outwardly tangible.
It will come, I know it.
And yet it creeps upon me like the acrid taste of the decomposed.
Reluctant hand shake,
Swift pat on the shoulder,
Fleeing embrace.
I will suffer the foul smell from the purity of intent,
The unbearable nonsense of external stimuli,
The awakening of the self,
the awareness of the same,
The putrid contentment of being alive.
For you, this, my fealty.
For you, this, my truth.
For you, this, my words.

It Was in a Dream

It was in a dream.
In the attic of my inner self,
The imagination of childhoods passed lies deep within the unconscious state,
Swept away by hurting memories
A surreal image of your eyes,
Deep set, a hue of cavernous black,
Uncovered and unveiled.
Your voice trespassed the dreaming realm and resonated
And as it spoke
I trembled and I wept, wept the tears not shed upon your altars.
You are my human heart who carries with my fusilladed sins,
Which has rendered me dogmatic.
I have become introvert.
I have been in a place, within a wakeful state,
A place where life loses its meaning and revolves in nothingness,
Where feeling becomes strange and pain second nature,
Where Life is a prison and Time the punisher, where there is no you.
And it was in a dream where I found you again,
And It was in a dream where I kept every part of you in reference to me.
